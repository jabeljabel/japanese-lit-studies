About This Visualization
Methodology

This visualization shows the co-paneling network at American Comparative Literature Association (ACLA) conferences. Nodes represent scholars; edges connect people who have appeared on the same panel or session. Node size reflects degree (number of unique co-panelists), and color indicates primary institutional affiliation.

Use the year slider to focus on specific time periods. The Top-N slider controls how many nodes are displayed (by degree). The edge weight filter removes weak connections.

Data Sources

Co-paneling edges are computed from ACLA presentation records in the project database. Two scholars share an edge if they appeared on the same session/panel in at least one year.

Network Layout

Force: D3.js v7 force simulation with adaptive parameters (weaker charge for larger networks). Timeline: X-axis fixed to first-appearance year.

Filtering

The Top-N slider selects the N highest-degree nodes in the year-filtered network. The institution filter can restrict to specific institutions.
